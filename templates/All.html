Acute Lymphoblastic Leukemia Classification

This project focuses on the classification of acute lymphoblastic leukemia (ALL) using deep learning techniques. By utilizing the fastai library, we develop a model that can accurately classify microscopic images of blood cells as either ALL-positive or ALL-negative. The dataset used for this project contains a collection of microscopic images of blood cells obtained from patients with acute lymphoblastic leukemia.

The model is trained on a large number of labeled images to learn the distinguishing features of ALL-positive and ALL-negative cells. It takes into account various characteristics of the cells, such as shape, size, and texture, to make accurate predictions.

About Acute Lymphoblastic Leukemia

Acute lymphoblastic leukemia (ALL) is a type of blood cancer that primarily affects lymphoid cells. It is characterized by the uncontrolled proliferation of immature lymphoblasts in the bone marrow and blood. ALL is the most common type of cancer in children, but it can also occur in adults.
