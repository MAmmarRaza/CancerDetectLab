Early Breast Cancer Detection

Analyze and classify breast histopathology images for invasive and non-invasive ductal carcinoma

Early Breast Cancer Detection

This project focuses on the detection and classification of early-stage breast cancer using machine learning techniques. Specifically, it aims to differentiate between invasive and non-invasive ductal carcinoma, which are two common types of breast cancer.

The dataset used for this project contains a collection of histopathology images of breast tissue samples. Each image is labeled as either invasive ductal carcinoma or non-invasive ductal carcinoma.

The machine learning model is trained on these labeled images to learn the distinguishing features of each type of cancer. It takes into account various characteristics of the tissue samples, such as cell structure, density, and arrangement, to accurately classify them as either invasive or non-invasive ductal carcinoma.

About Invasive and Non-Invasive Ductal Carcinoma

Invasive ductal carcinoma is a type of breast cancer that starts in the milk ducts but has spread to surrounding breast tissue. It is the most common type of breast cancer. Non-invasive ductal carcinoma, also known as ductal carcinoma in situ (DCIS), is a type of breast cancer that remains confined to the milk ducts and has not spread to surrounding tissue.

  • Early detection of breast cancer can significantly improve treatment outcomes.
  • Invasive ductal carcinoma is characterized by the cancer cells breaking through the ductal walls and spreading to surrounding tissues.
  • Non-invasive ductal carcinoma remains confined to the milk ducts and has not spread to surrounding tissues.
  • Timely diagnosis and appropriate treatment are crucial for better prognosis.
  • Regular breast cancer screening and awareness can help detect cancer at an early stage.
